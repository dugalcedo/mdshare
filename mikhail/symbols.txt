Brackets
()      parentheses (US), or round brackets (UK)
[]      square brackets
{}      curly brckets
<>      angle brackets

Punctuation
.       period or dot
,       comma
;       semicolon
:       colon
-       dash or hyphen (but minus in math)
!       exclamation point or exclamation mark
?       question mark

Quotes and accent marks
"       double quote (US), or double speech-mark (UK)
'       apostrophe, or single quote (US), or single speech-mark(UK)
`       backtic
        (only programmers call it this.
        Otherwise, most english speakers don't know)

Most English speakers don't know the names of these either
~       tilde
^       caret
¨       umlaut

Other
|       pipe
/       slash, or forward slash
\       backslash
_       underscore
*       star or asterisk
@       at-sign or at-symbol
#       number sign, hashtag, or pound sign (old-fashioned)
%       percent (but modulus or modulo in programming)
&       and-sign or ampersand

Math
        Name of symbol      Name of mathematical operation
=       equals-sign
+       plus                addition
-       minus               subtraction
/       slash               division
÷       division sign       division
        (not used in programming)
*       star or asterisk    multiplication
×       times               multiplication
        (not used in programming)

Comparison
<       less-than
<=      less-than-or-equal-to
>       greater-than
>=      greater-than-or-equal-to
==      is-equal-to
        (is-loosely-equal-to in javascript/typescript)
!=      is-not-equal-to
        (is-not-loosely-equal-to in javascript/typescript)
===     is-strictly-equal-to
        (javascript/typescript only)
!==     is-not-strictly-equal-to
        (javascript/typescript only)
